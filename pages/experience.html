Planned Bachelor Thesis at SBB

(Autumn 2026)

In my bachelor thesis, I investigate uncertainty quantification methods for train occupancy forecasting. The goal is to construct calibrated confidence intervals around occupancy predictions in order to estimate prediction uncertainty.

These uncertainty estimates can support the Swiss Federal Railways (SBB) in operational decision-making, for example when planning train capacity and the number of wagons required and I will work with real world data.

The project is based on real-world operational data provided by the SBB.
